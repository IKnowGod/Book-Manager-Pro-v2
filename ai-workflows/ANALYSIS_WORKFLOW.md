# Analysis Order of Operations

To get the most accurate results for your Chapter Timeline, Subway Map, and Continuity Checks, follow this recommended workflow when adding new content:

## 1. Character & Detail Tagging (Metadata First)
Before scanning chapters, ensure your characters and world details are defined.
*   **Action**: Open your Character or Detail notes.
*   **Step**: Click `✦ AI Suggest Tags`.
*   **Why**: This populates the master tag list with your character names. Our system uses these tags to deterministically track character presence in chapters.

## 2. Chapter Content Scanning (Tagging)
Once your characters are tagged, scan your chapter text.
*   **Action**: Open a Chapter note.
*   **Step**: Click `✦ AI Suggest Tags`.
*   **Why**: This will automatically find your characters in the text and tag the chapter with their names. This is what feeds the **Chapter Timeline**.

## 3. Establishing Relationships (Related Notes)
Now that tags are set, formalize the links between notes.
*   **Action**: In the same Chapter note, scroll to `Related Notes`.
*   **Step**: Use the `Shared Tags` suggestions (deterministic) or click `✦ AI Suggest` for broader AI scanning.
*   **Why**: This creates the explicit "Character Mention" links required for the **Narrative Subway Map (Thread Tracker)**.

## 4. Continuity Verification
Check for contradictions against your established lore.
*   **Action**: Click `✦ Run AI Check` in the Consistency Check section.
*   **Why**: It scans your content against mentioned character notes and previous chapters to find "disconnected" facts (e.g., eye color changes, timeline slips).

## 5. Global Analysis & Reporting
Finally, head to the **Analysis Hub** to see the big picture.
*   **Action**: Run Themes, Pacing, and Subway Map analysis.
*   **Why**: Since you've already established the "ground truth" through tagging and linking, the AI reports will be much more accurate and grounded in your specific story facts.

---
> [!TIP]
> **Deterministic vs AI**: Always prioritize using the "Shared Tags" and "Mentioned in Text" indicators. These are grounded in your actual text and are more reliable than one-off AI guesses.
